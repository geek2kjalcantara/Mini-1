Basics of Baseball

Baseball players are some of the most athletic and skilled athletes in the world. They must be able to hit a ball thrown at speeds of up to 100 miles per hour, run the bases at full speed, and make difficult catches in the field. Baseball players also need to have a strong mental game, as they must be able to make quick decisions and stay focused under pressure.

Message from MLB Players

What the Players Say

Mike Trout

@miketrout27

Gamesmanship is a trait that can work well with both pitchers and quarterbacks. Fans may feel that catchers and pitchers hang out and spend time with each other off the field. Not really and I will tell you why. Catchers come to the plate as batters in the line-up 3–4 times a game. And with players transitioning from team to team quite often these days a former teammate like a catcher can provide his new mates with inside information on what and when they may see a certain pitch during an at-bat. Hitting can be an uplifting experience when you know what to expect from a hurler.

Carlos Correa

@carloscorrea04

Unlike most other sports, baseball exists outside of time — there is no clock to control game play or judge a player's performance. Nevertheless, a dizzying array of statistics record nearly every aspect of the game in amazing detail, and allow fans (and team managers!) to compare the performance of one player against another.

Sandy Alcantara

@sandyalcantara22

The game is played on a diamond-shaped playing field, the four corners of the diamond being formed by home plate, first base, second base and third base. In the middle of the infield is the pitcher's mound, where the pitcher stands to pitch the ball to the batter. The area beyond the infield, bordered by the first and third baselines, is called the outfield.!
